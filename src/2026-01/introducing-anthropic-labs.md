# Introducing Labs: Anthropic's New Experimental Products Division

**Source**: https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-anthropic-labs

**Date**: January 13, 2026

**Author**: Anthropic

**Keywords**: Anthropic, Labs, AI products, Claude, experimental products, Mike Krieger, organizational structure

## Elevator pitch

Anthropic expands Labs as a dedicated division for incubating experimental AI products at the frontier of Claude's capabilities, led by Instagram co-founder Mike Krieger alongside Ben Mann.

## Takeaways

- Labs is a structural shift in how Anthropic develops and scales AI products, providing room to experiment before scaling responsibly
- Mike Krieger (Instagram co-founder) joins Labs to work alongside Ben Mann, while Ami Vora leads the broader Product organization
- Claude Code grew from research preview to a billion-dollar product in just six months through this experimental approach
- Model Context Protocol (MCP) reached 100 million monthly downloads and became an industry standard
- Anthropic is actively recruiting builders with track records in creating beloved products and shaping emerging technology

## Synthesis

Anthropic has announced a significant organizational evolution with the expansion of Labs, a team dedicated to incubating experimental products at the frontier of Claude's capabilities. This announcement signals a maturation in how the company approaches product development, recognizing that the rapid pace of AI advancement demands new organizational structures and methodologies.

The leadership appointments are particularly notable. Mike Krieger, co-founder of Instagram and former Chief Product Officer, brings extensive experience in building consumer-scale products. His joining Labs alongside Ben Mann suggests Anthropic is serious about translating cutting-edge AI research into polished, widely-adopted products. Meanwhile, Ami Vora, who joined Anthropic in late 2025, will lead the broader Product organization in partnership with CTO Rahul Patil to scale Claude experiences for millions of users. This dual structure—experimental Labs and scaling-focused Product—mirrors successful organizational patterns seen at other technology companies.

Daniela Amodei, President of Anthropic, articulated the rationale clearly: the speed of AI advancement demands a different approach to building, organizing, and resource allocation. Labs provides the freedom to experiment and discover what works before committing to responsible scaling. This philosophy acknowledges that in a rapidly evolving field, premature optimization or over-engineering can be counterproductive.

The track record of products emerging from this experimental mindset is impressive. Claude Code, which started as a research preview, grew into a billion-dollar product within six months—a remarkable trajectory that validates the Labs approach. Perhaps even more significant is the Model Context Protocol (MCP), which achieved 100 million monthly downloads and established itself as an industry standard. MCP's success demonstrates that Anthropic's experimental products can shape the broader AI ecosystem, not just Anthropic's own offerings.

Recent launches including Skills, Claude in Chrome, and Cowork further demonstrate the agentic capabilities being developed within this framework. These products represent different points on the spectrum from experimental to production-ready, illustrating the pipeline from Labs innovation to scaled deployment.

The announcement also includes a clear hiring call for builders with proven track records in creating beloved products while shaping emerging technology responsibly. This emphasis on responsibility alongside innovation reflects Anthropic's continued focus on AI safety even as it accelerates product development. The company appears to be building a team that can move fast while maintaining the thoughtful approach to AI deployment that has characterized Anthropic's public positioning.

This organizational structure positions Anthropic to compete more effectively in the increasingly competitive AI landscape while maintaining its research-driven identity. By separating experimental work from scaling efforts, the company can pursue bold ideas without the constraints of immediate commercial viability, while still having a clear path to bring successful experiments to market.
